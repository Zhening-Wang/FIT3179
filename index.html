Police Brutality in the United States

The following visualisation illustrates the police brutality issue in the US,
especially the unfair actions against coloured minorities.

Overview of US Police Shooting from 2015 to 2020

The stacked bar chart depicts the number of police shooting by human race across the US. The time frame is from 2015 to 2020. Moreover, arguably the most famous "Breonna Taylor" case is explicitly listed out.

Police Brutality
in a Nutshell

Nowadays, the term 'police brutality' has gone viral on the internet. People have been calling the police's actions as 'brutality' because sometimes their unfair actions were targeted against unarmed people. The polar chart shows the number of police shooting against unarmed people from 2015 to 2020.







Police Brutality Against Coloured Minorities

Among all US police brutality cases from 2015 to 2020, people have been furious about some cases which were targeted toward unarmed, coloured minorities. These events have escalated quickly and have caused some significant effect on society.

For instance, the percentage of unarmed shooting victims being 'black' is almost three times the percentage of black population in the US. Whereas for 'white' Americans, the percentage of being an unarmed shooting victim is two-thirds of the percentage of white population in the US. Such inequality clearly implies the unfair actions against coloured minorities.
